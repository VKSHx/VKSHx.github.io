1. Time-block your deep work

Block 90-minute windows in the morning for focused, uninterrupted work. Research on cognitive performance consistently shows that the brain operates best on complex tasks before afternoon. Guard these blocks aggressively.

2. Process email twice a day

Most professionals check email over 70 times per day. Restricting yourself to two scheduled sessions recovers roughly 90 minutes of productive time daily.

3. Use a weekly review

Every Friday afternoon, spend 20 minutes reviewing what you completed, what slipped, and what matters most next week.

4. Eat the frog

Identify the task you are most likely to avoid and do it first. The psychological weight of procrastination costs more energy than the task itself.

5. Protect the last hour

Treat the final hour of your workday as a shutdown ritual — clear inboxes, update your task list, note tomorrow's top three priorities, and close all tabs.
