The AI Investment Bubble: Myth or Reality?

By James Okonkwo · May 2026

Markets have been here before. The late 1990s saw extraordinary capital flow into internet companies, followed by an equally extraordinary correction. Today, a new question hangs over every earnings call: is AI spending a rational bet on transformative technology, or the latest iteration of a recurring pattern?

The bulls have real data to cite. Cloud revenue tied to AI workloads is growing faster than any comparable technology wave. Foundation model capabilities have advanced substantially in 18 months. Enterprise adoption, though patchy, is measurable in labour-hour savings.

The bears have questions the data does not yet answer. Most productivity gains are concentrated in narrow task categories — code completion, summarisation, classification. Broader reasoning tasks, where the largest labour value sits, remain unreliable at scale.

The most honest answer is that nobody knows. What history suggests is that even correct technology bets often require a correction before reaching their true valuation. Positioning for that scenario is not the same as calling the technology wrong.
