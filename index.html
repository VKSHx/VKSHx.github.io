The attack explained

Indirect prompt injection in 4 steps

A user asks their AI assistant to summarise a webpage. The page looks normal. But hidden in the HTML is an instruction the LLM will follow.

1

Attacker plants a payload

Malicious instructions are embedded in a webpage — white text on white background, a 1px font, or a clipped div invisible to human readers.

2

User asks the AI to summarise

A real user sends a legitimate request: "Summarise this article for me." The AI fetches or receives the page content.

3

LLM sees the hidden text

The text extractor includes all text regardless of CSS visibility. The LLM reads the injected instructions alongside the real content.

4

Guardrail blocks — or it doesn't

With a guardrail agent in place, the injection is detected before the LLM processes it. Without one, the AI follows the attacker's instructions.
